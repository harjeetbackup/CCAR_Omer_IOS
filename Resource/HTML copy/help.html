HOME SCREEN MENU:

● All Cards – Tap to see a list of all of the readings in order. Tap any day to see the reading for that day. You can navigate to the next or previous readings by swiping side to side or by tapping the arrow buttons at the bottom.

This menu item also displays the percentage of cards marked as "read". To mark a card as "read", tap the box with the check mark at the bottom of each reading card. It will change from white to orange to indicate it's marked as read.

● Bookmarks & Notes – Tap to show a list of all reading cards that have been bookmarked and/or that have notes added by you. Tap on the day to go to each card. From there, to see the voice or text notes, tap on the note icon at the bottom of the card, and select "Voice Notes" or "Text Notes" to see the note(s).

● Today's Reading – Tap for the appropriate daily reading. The app will automatically input the current date and you must select if it is before sunset or after, so you can be taken to the correct reading for that day. In Jewish tradition, each day begins at sundown. You can also select a different date to see the reading for that day.

Weekly Theme – Each week's readings have an overarching theme, listed in the menu. You can browse by week and theme.

BOTTOM BUTTONS:

Search - Find words within any of the readings. The resulting list will contain all cards on which the searched-for word or phrase can be found. Tap on a card to see the word highlighted in context within the reading.

Settings – This can be used to quickly and easily remove all bookmarks, all voice notes, or all text notes. Tap "Reset Application" to remove all of these at once, and restore the initial condition of the app. Please use these carefully as this cannot be undone.

Help – Tapping the question mark will bring you to this help document.

Information – This displays important information about the application, such as the creators of the content and the app, as well as copyright information.

ON THE READING CARDS:

Flip Card – Tap to reveal the Omer blessing on the back of the card.
